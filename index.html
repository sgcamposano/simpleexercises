Reach Your Limit And Get To The Next Level

Exercise is defined as any bodily activity that enhances or maintains physical fitness, overall health, and wellness. It is performed to aid growth, improve strength, prevent aging, develop muscles and the cardiovascular system, hone athletic skills, lose weight, and providing enjoyment. Regular exercise reduces the risk of chronic diseases, improves respiration, and enhances blood circulation.

Types of Physical Exercises

Aerobic Exercise

Uses large muscle groups and increases oxygen consumption to improve cardiovascular endurance.

Running Swimming Cycling

Anaerobic Exercise

Includes strength and resistance training to build muscle mass, bone density, and balance.

WT Sprinting HIIT

Flexibility Exercise

Stretches and lengthens muscles to improve range of motion and reduce injury risk.

Leg Stretches Split Arm Stretch

Phases of a Fitness Program

Warm-up

The initial phase (5-10 minutes) elevates body temperature and prepares muscles to prevent cramps and injuries. It leads to increased flexibility, blood flow, and improved performance.

Exercise Proper

The phase where the individual performs the specific program to meet training objectives. This is the main part of the workout focusing on your goals.

Cool Down

Gradual tapering off to prevent dizziness and blood pooling by keeping blood circulating for recovery. Helps return heart rate to normal levels.

Principles of Training

Specificity

Exercises must duplicate the movements and energy systems of the desired activity.

Overload

The body must be exercised beyond its accustomed level to adapt and grow stronger.

Progression

Intensity must increase gradually over time to avoid injury while ensuring improvement.

Reversibility

Fitness is lost if exercise stops. "Use it or lose it."

Tedium

Variations should be introduced to avoid boredom and prevent plateaus in progress.

Individualization

Programs must cater to unique individual needs, goals, and physiological differences.

The F.I.T.T. Formula

Frequency

How often one exercises (e.g., 3-5 times a week).

Intensity

How hard the activity is (e.g., weight lifted, heart rate).

Time

How long the activity lasts (e.g., 15-30 minutes).

Type

The specific kind of activity chosen (e.g., Cardio, Strength).
